# GTM (Go-To-Market) Strategy
**AI Spend Auditor**

## Exact Target User
**Title:** VP of Engineering / CTO / Head of Operations
**Company Stage:** Series A or Series B funded startups (20–150 employees). 
**Why:** At this stage, they have enough employees that individual AI tool subscriptions (Cursor, ChatGPT Plus, Copilot) are expensed organically and have spiraled out of centralized control. They are burning $1k–$5k/month on AI tools but lack a procurement team to audit it.

## The Trigger (Right Before They Want This)
- They are reviewing the monthly Ramp/Brex corporate card statement and see "OpenAI", "Anthropic", "GitHub", "Cursor" scattered across 20 different employee cards.
- A finance review meeting where the CFO asks, "Why are we paying for both Cursor and Copilot for the engineering team?"
- They search: "How to manage startup SaaS spend", "Cursor vs Copilot cost", "GitHub Copilot team vs enterprise pricing".

## Where They Hang Out Online
- **Communities:** r/SaaS, r/startup, Hacker News, YC Startup School forums, specific Slack communities like CTO Craft or Rands Leadership Slack.
- **Content consumption:** The Pragmatic Engineer newsletter, Lenny's Newsletter, specific X (Twitter) lists of technical founders.

## First 100 Users in 30 Days ($0 Budget)
**Not "content marketing." Specific actions:**
1. **The "Ramp/Brex Statement Analysis" Cold Email:** Find 50 Series A CTOs on LinkedIn. Send a highly specific cold email: *"Hey [Name], I'm building a tool that audits AI subscription waste. In my research, companies your size (50 devs) usually overpay by $1.2k/mo because engineers expense both Cursor and Copilot, or use ChatGPT Team for users who just need Plus. Mind running your stack through this free auditor (no signup required) to see if it catches anything for [Company]?"*
2. **The Hacker News "Show HN":** Title: *Show HN: I built an auditor that tells you which AI tools your team is overpaying for.* The hook is controversy: developers love their tools, but founders hate the duplicate spend. The comments will debate Cursor vs Copilot; the tool will get traffic.
3. **The "Pragmatic Engineer" Reply:** Gergely Orosz frequently posts about developer tools. Reply to a relevant thread with a screenshot of the tool's output showing the exact overlap cost of Copilot + Windsurf.
4. **Indie Hackers / X Build-in-Public:** Share the exact deterministic rules engine. "Here is the math on why upgrading 3 users from Claude Pro to Claude Team is actually a waste of money."

## The Unfair Distribution Channel
**Credex's existing network and credibility.**
Credex already talks to startups about discounted compute and infrastructure. This tool becomes the ultimate "free assessment" before a sales call. Sales reps can say: "Before we talk about discounts, just drop your team's current tools into this link. It'll take 2 minutes and show you if you even need our help." It weaponizes the sales process into a self-serve consultative audit.

## Week-1 Traction Metrics (If Successful)
- **Unique Visitors:** 1,500 (driven primarily by the Show HN or a viral X post).
- **Audits Completed:** 300 (20% conversion from visitor to completing the form, achievable due to no pre-gate).
- **Leads Captured:** 45 (15% of those who complete the audit choose to enter their email for the report/Credex consultation).
- **Consultations Booked:** 5.
