# Go-to-Market Strategy

## Target Persona
Our exact target user is a **Fractional CTO or Series A Engineering Manager**. 
- **Why?** They manage budgets ranging from $1k–$5k/month for software tools but don't have dedicated procurement teams. They are technical enough to understand what "Cursor" and "Copilot" do, but busy enough that they don't have time to cross-reference invoices to see who is using both. 
- **Pain Point:** They know they are bleeding cash on abandoned AI subscriptions but lack visibility.

## $0 Budget Acquisition Channels

### 1. The "Roast My Stack" X/Twitter Campaign
- **Strategy:** I will post a thread showing how a "standard" 10-person dev team wastes exactly $3,400 a year on overlapping AI tools (e.g., buying Copilot for everyone while devs quietly expense Cursor). 
- **Execution:** Instead of asking people to sign up for a newsletter, the call-to-action is: *"Drop a screenshot of your team's AI stack below, and I'll run it through the audit engine and reply with your exact wasted spend."* This leverages public engagement. Once they see the number, I DM them the link to the live app to get the full report.

### 2. Micro-Tool Sidecar for VCs
- **Strategy:** Venture Capital associates are constantly trying to add value to their portfolio companies (PortCos) without being annoying. 
- **Execution:** I will cold-email 20 junior VC associates with a white-labeled or dedicated tracking link. The pitch: *"Send this AI Spend Auditor to your PortCos. It takes 30 seconds, requires no login, and will instantly show them how to extend their runway by cutting redundant AI tools."* This turns the VCs into a free distribution channel because it makes *them* look helpful to founders.
